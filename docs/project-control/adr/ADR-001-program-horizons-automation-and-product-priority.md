# ADR-001 — Program Horizons, Automation, And Product Priority

## Status
Approved program direction; not implemented by this ADR.

## Related Owner Decision IDs
D1, D2, D12, D13, D14.

## Context
Program targets, automation percentages, California priority, Law & Business, C-10, and beta timing can conflict unless evidence gates are explicit.

## Decision
Use three horizons. Treat the twenty-four-month California objective and fourteen-day beta as conditional targets. Define 95% automation as eligible routine steps only. Law & Business is first; C-10 follows after its gates.

## Alternatives Considered
Fixed-date nationwide rollout; zero-human-action automation; C-10 first; immediate beta.

## Rejected Alternatives
Rejected because they bypass evidence, source, risk, or protected human gates.

## Consequences
Targets remain ambitious but claims and sequencing are evidence-bound.

## Risks
Scope pressure, denominator manipulation, and target language being mistaken for guarantees.

## Controls
Milestone evidence, explicit eligible-step denominator, claim review, and owner triggers.

## Implementation Prerequisites
Separate product, content, research, evaluation, and release gates.

## Non-Authorized Scope
No product module, content, release, public claim, or automation service is authorized.

## Validation Requirements
Trace each milestone and automation claim to current evidence and retained human controls.

## Supersession Links
Supersedes no historical phase record. Later changes require an explicit ADR link.
